Key findings & recommended actions
🔴 Response time crisis — 4× slowdown in 3 weeks
Average response time jumped from 114ms (May 6–12) to 495ms (May 28–Jun 3), peaking at 696ms on May 28. This directly suppresses crawl rate — Google backs off when your server is slow. Run a server-level audit: check hosting load, memory, database queries, and any new middleware added in late May.
🔴 149 pages "discovered but not indexed" — Google sees them, won't index them
This is your biggest indexation problem. These are likely the ~78 culled articles from your May 2026 recovery that now have 301 redirects, thin content, or canonical issues. Audit them in GSC URL Inspection. Pages not indexed after being crawled usually have thin content, duplicate signals, or soft 404 behavior.
🔴 49 redirect pages failed validation
The 301 redirects you set up during the content cull appear to be misfiring. Failed redirect validation means Google is encountering redirect chains, redirect loops, or redirects to non-200 pages. Audit each redirect destination — make sure they land on live, indexable 200-response pages.
🟡 91.2% of crawls are "refresh" — Google not discovering new pages
Only 8.8% of crawl budget goes to discovering new content. This confirms Googlebot is in maintenance mode on your site rather than expanding coverage. Submit your updated sitemap (your 20 canonical guides) and use URL Inspection to request indexing on priority pages manually.
🟡 72% of crawls by Desktop Googlebot — mobile coverage lagging
Only 5.6% of crawls are Smartphone Googlebot, which is very low for a mobile-first indexed site. This suggests your mobile rendering or mobile sitemap signals may be weaker than desktop. Check if your mobile pages have the same canonical tags and structured data as desktop.
🟡 www vs non-www split — 44 requests hitting www subdomain
Similar to tradeconvert.pro, both wtkpro.site (18,047) and www.wtkpro.site (44) are being crawled. Confirm a clean 301 redirect from www → non-www (or vice versa) and set your preferred domain in GSC settings.
🟢 92.7% success rate — solid, much better than tradeconvert
Only 2% 404 rate (17 pages) vs tradeconvert's 15.4%. The content cull and redirect work has clearly reduced broken pages. The remaining 17 need to be found and either restored or redirected properly.
🟢 Indexation is growing — from 3 to 206 pages in 3 weeks
Coverage went from just 3 indexed pages on May 8 to 206 by May 29. The recovery overhaul is working. The trajectory is positive — the main job now is clearing the 149 "discovered not indexed" backlog and fixing the redirect validation failures.
